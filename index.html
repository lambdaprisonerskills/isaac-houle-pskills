Why We Care

There are an estimated 5 million formerly incarcerated people living in the U.S., and approximately 27% of those people are unemployed. That's nearly 5X the unployment rate of the general United States population.

What We Do

Prison employees build profiles for their most marketable inmates. Employers can then see those profiles and hire conscientious, skillful, dependable people for cheap.

Did You Know?

In a study by Johns Hopkins Hospitals, employees with a criminal background had a lower turnover rate than those with no criminal background.

What Next

The majority of people incarcerated in federal prisons are convicted of non-violent crimes. Almost 11,000 youth are imprisoned for offenses that aren't even crimes. Prisoners in the U.S. get a bad rap, but studies show they are willing to work, and they work hard.

Ready to Find Work?

Less than half of the U.S. prison population is employed. If you're a prison administrator who is ready to help your residents find good work, sign up or login below to get started.
